A Look Behind the Numbers

The 2000s have more highly rated movies than the 1990s, and the 2010s have more highly rated movies
than the 2000s. The numbers at face value suggest people have gotten better at making movies. However,
the increase in the number of highly rated movies can be explained by other factors.

The Number of Movies Released

IMDb estimates there were 15,465 movies released between 1990 and 1999 compared to 49,916 movies
released between 2010 and 2019. The increase in the number of movies released is significantly more
than the increase in the number of highly rated movies, which suggests that people have gotten worse
at making movies, not better. However, this is also an unfair assessment because a substantial
percentage of the movies released between 2010 and 2019 were made with limited financing. Advancements
in technology have made it easier to produce low-budget movies, but it's unreasonable to compare these films
to big studio productions. Nevertheless, the consensus is that the number of high-budget films has increased
each decade, but it's difficult to quantify the increase with limited access to the data.

Shifting Market Demographics and the Rise of the Superhero Movie

Our bar chart illustrates a big jump in the number of highly rated action movies released, and a notable
drop in the number of highly rated comedy movies released. Both of these occurrences can be explained
by the movie industry's shifting market demographics. The international market has grown significantly for
the major film studios, and it's more profitable to produce films that appeal to a wider audience. Hence,
the rise of the superhero movie. With exciting fight scenes, spectacular special effects, outrageous evil
villians, and larger-than-life superheroes, it's easy to see why superhero movies have universal appeal.
Superhero movies fall into the action category, and they tend to be highly rated on IMDb. On the other hand,
the comedy genre has suffered due to the growth of the international market. Everyone loves to laugh, but
there are cultural differences in humor, and it's easy for things to get lost in translation. Therefore,
less comedies are being made resulting in a decrease in the number of highly rated comedies.
