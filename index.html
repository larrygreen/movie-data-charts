A Look Behind the Numbers

The 2000s have more highly rated movies than the 1990s, and the 2010s have more highly rated movies
than the 2000s. The numbers at face value suggest people have gotten better at making movies. However,
the increase in the number of highly rated movies can be explained by other factors, like the number of
movies being made. IMDb estimates there were 15,465 movies released between 1990 and 1999 compared to 49,916 movies released between 2010 and 2019. The increase in the number of movies released is significantly
more than the increase in the number of highly rated movies released, which suggests that people have
gotten worse at making movies, not better. However, this is also an unfair assessment because a substantial
percentage of the movies released between 2010 and 2019 were made with limited financing. Advancements in
technology have made it easier to produce low-budget movies, but it's unreasonable to compare these films
to big studio productions
